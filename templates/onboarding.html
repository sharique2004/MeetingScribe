Welcome to MeetingScribe

Record any meeting, get a speaker-labelled transcript with live captions, and an AI summary — everything runs on this Mac. Audio and transcripts never leave it unless you choose to sync a meeting to your phone. A minute of setup makes it all work.

Read meetings on your phone — optional

Sign in once and any meeting you toggle “View on phone” becomes readable at 5uh76ypz.insforge.site — transcript and summary text only, never audio. Skip this and MeetingScribe is 100% offline.

Connect your calendar

Recordings name themselves after the meeting happening right now, and you get a “Join & record?” reminder when an event starts. Works with every calendar your Mac knows — for Google Calendar, add your Google account under System Settings → Internet Accounts and tick Calendars.

Audio devices

Your mic records “You”. To also capture the other side of online calls, the free BlackHole driver is needed once: brew install blackhole-2ch (or re-run bash setup.sh).

Summaries, transcript tidy-up and the practice interviewer run on the Neural Engine through Apple Intelligence — nothing goes to the cloud.

Notifications

MeetingScribe nudges you to record when a calendar event starts or when it notices you're in a call, and tells you when a transcript is ready. macOS asks for permission the first time the app runs — choose Allow. Change it any time in System Settings → Notifications.

Every step above can be finished later — nothing here is required.
